Our Story

July 2025

Founded in Bangalore by Meghna Chaudhary, Harsh Snehanshu and Shruti Sah, Pint of View began as a weekend project to bring knowledge out of the four walls of strict academic spaces into bars and informal venues. Inspired by a Pint of Science in the UK & Lectures on Tap in the US and tired of waiting for someone to start something like this, the three decided to kickstart Pint of View in India, and very soon, it became a triweekly ritual for Bangalore, and a weekly habit for the rest of the country.

Meghna is a machine learning engineer, and is now pursuing her Master's at University College London. Harsh & Shruti are serial community builders and entrepreneurs. They started Cubbon Reads and Bookmark — a dating app for readers , prior to this.

What started in Bangalore has spawned into a pan-India movement with chapters in 13+ cities.

Now expanding globally with our first international chapter in London.

Philosophy

Pint of View began as a protest against utilitarian learning.

You remember that feeling? That childlike curiosity that made you fall into rabbit holes of nerdiness for no reason at all.

Yeah. That died the moment learning became utilitarian. Entrance exams. Jobs. Startup funding. Everything became a means to an end. And the end just kept moving.

We started Pint of View as a protest against that.

Our lectures have no tangible ROI. No promotions. No placements. Just ideas, curated well enough to pull you in.

We find professors who make you care. Who make you learn for the pure unadulterated joy of learning.

Not so you can get a job. Not so you can sound smarter on a date. But so you go home with questions. So you Google at 2 am and fall into your own rabbit hole again.

Like the first time you heard about black holes or read Shakespeare.

We're here to make you become the polymath you’ve always wanted to be, without knowing.

What We Do

Curated Lectures

Thoughtfully selected topics and speakers who bring deep knowledge and passion to every session. We work primarily with university professors, most of them referred by our previous speakers.

Relaxed Spaces With a Pint!

Bars, coworking spaces, and bookstores — informal venues that encourage open dialogue over drinks with no power dynamic.

Learning for Joy

No hidden agendas. Pure intellectual curiosity and the simple pleasure of expanding your mind.
